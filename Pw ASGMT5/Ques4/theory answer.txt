Q1. What is SEO and explain the importance of SEO?

Answer -
SEO stands for Search Engine Optimization. It helps you optimize your websites so that they have better
rankings on Search Engines like Google, Bing, and Yahoo.
The ultimate goal of SEO is to rank higher in search engine results pages for relevant keywords and phrases,
which can increase visibility, traffic, and ultimately revenue for the website.
The importance of SEO -
Meta tags are important for SEO because they provide information to search engines about the content on your
site and can help improve your website's visibility and ranking in search engine results pages.


Q3. What are the Benefits of Using Meta Tags

1--Improved SEO: Although meta tags are not the sole determinant of a page's search engine ranking, they can still contribute to better visibility on search engine results pages (SERPs). The meta title and description tags, in particular, can influence click-through rates, which indirectly affect SEO.

2--Increased Click-Through Rates (CTR): A well-crafted meta title and description can entice users to click on your page in search results. If the meta tags accurately describe the page's content and appeal to the searcher's intent, it can lead to higher CTR and more organic traffic.

3--Enhanced User Experience: Meta tags provide concise information about the page's content. This helps users quickly determine if the page is relevant to their query, improving the overall user experience. If the meta tags are misleading or poorly written, users may leave the page quickly, increasing bounce rates.

4--Better Branding: Crafting compelling meta titles and descriptions allows you to showcase your brand personality and unique selling points, helping to build brand recognition and trust among users.

5--Social Sharing: Some meta tags, such as Open Graph tags for Facebook and Twitter Card tags, affect how your content appears when shared on social media platforms. They can help ensure that your shared content looks visually appealing and displays the right information, encouraging more engagement.


Q5. Define Favicon and give an illustrative example.


Solution -
A favicon that stands for a favorite icon is a tiny icon included along with a website, which is displayed in places
like the browser's address bar, page tabs, and bookmarks menu. It is usually 16 * 16 pixels in size and stored in
GIF, PNG, or ICO file format.
